Word of God


Written by Mike Holohan


We need to understand the importance of the word of God; God’s word is His will.


In Gen 1v1 the first time we see God what is he doing; he is speaking and creating, he is

making his will known and he is bringing it to pass with his words. We see what God uses His

word for. We also see the divine order of God and it is reflected in his creation.


When God created the universe he didn’t just think to himself … I think I will make something…

maybe a little light over there… a few planets over there and then just click his fingers… no! He

spoke his will into existence with power.


What do we learn from how God created the universe? Gen 1v1ff.

1.    The words of God are containers, they contain his power, when God said let there be light

there was light, the light was created and there was enough power to sustain the light from the

day God created it to the present day.

Heb 1:3 upholding all things by the word of his power. God’s word is an expression of his

power. It is not the power of his word but the word of his power, or his power is released in

words. All things are held together by the word of his power.

2.    God’s words also contain his will, God wanted to create something and it was expressed in

words. There’s no room for idle words when your words contain power.

3.    God’s word contained his authority. He spoke and everything he spoke to obeyed. When

God spoke he spoke with power, can you imagine every time God said let there be… everything

in the universe rushed to carry out his commands.


God’s word still has as much power as it had when God spoke it as it is still working. This is one

thing we really need to learn… God’s word contains God’s power, it still contains the same

power as the first day he spoke it and if we learn how to use it right we can experience the

power of God in our lives… Rom 1:16 the gospel/good news is the power of God unto

salvation…


Ps 33:6 By the word of the LORD were the heavens made; and all the host of them by the

breath of his mouth.


Ps 33:9 For he spake, and it was done; he commanded, and it stood fast.

Isa 55:10-11 For as the rain cometh down, and the snow from heaven, and returneth not thither,

but watereth the earth, and maketh it bring forth and bud, that it may give seed to the sower,

and bread to the eater: So shall my word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return

unto me void, but it shall accomplish that which I please, and it shall prosper in the thing

whereto I sent it. God’s word is not absent of power it just needs to be spoken.


We discovered God’s words are full of power, they will accomplish everything that God sends

them to do but can God’s word be trusted, we know God has the power but will he do what he

said he would do.


Num 23:19, Heb 6:18, Titus 1:2 God cannot lie.


1Kings 8:56 Blessed be the LORD, that hath given rest unto his people Israel, according to all

that he promised: there hath not failed one word of all his good promise, which he promised by

the hand of Moses his servant. God’s word cannot fail.


Ps 119:89 It is forever settled (stands firm, unmovable)


Ps 138:2 God has exalted his word above his name.


1 Pet 1:23-25 God’s word is incorruptible.


God cannot lie, the whole creation is upheld by the integrity of God’s word, if God were ever to

lie then the whole world would collapse. Heb 1v3.


Ps 33:4 God’s word is right.

Ps 119:140, Prov 30:5 God’s word is pure (refined).


God’s testimony


Prov 8:8 all the words of my mouth are in righteousness; there is nothing froward (crooked) or

perverse (false) in them. They are all plain to him that understandeth, and right to them that find

knowledge. God is saying here that he only tells the truth, there is nothing crooked or false

about his words


Matt 24:35 Heaven and earth shall pass away, but my words shall not pass away.


Isa 55:10-11 God’s word will always prosper in the thing he sent it for.


Ps 89:34 God doesn’t break or alter his word.


Jer 33:20-21 If you can break his covenant with day and night then his covenant with us can be

broken.


Isa 45:22-23 God’s word shall not return empty.


One of the mistakes people make concerning the word of God is that they substitute the

integrity of God for with the sovereignty of God and they get confused.

Some people have a distorted view of God's sovereignty which basically says, "God is

sovereign, Therefore, whatever happens to me in life must be God's will for me."

Just recently I read about a Christian man that starved to death in his truck in the mountains of

Oregon. He got stuck in a snow drift in a mountain pass and sat in that truck for nine weeks

waiting for someone to rescue Him "if it was the Lord's will."

Throughout that time he kept a diary and wrote letters to loved ones. It is clear from those

diaries that this man believed God caused Him to get stuck in that snow and that he would get

out only if it was God's will. For more than two months He sat there wondering whether or not it

was God's will for Him to live. Finally, he died of starvation.

When someone finally found Him, he was only a 100 yard walk away from a clear road he could

have used to find help; 100 yards of snow and a wrong idea about God stood between this man

and life.

Starvation didn't kill that man. His theology did.

The sovereignty of God teaching that is rampant today basically says that God can do whatever

he wants, whenever he wants and we have no choice but to go along with it, but the integrity of

God says that God can only do what he said he would do.


God has limited his sovereignty by his word. God will only do what he said he will do in his

word…or he has given us his word/promise…


For example:


Gen 8:22 While the earth remaineth, seedtime and harvest, and cold and heat, and summer

and winter, and day and night shall not cease. Can God then change his mind?


Gen 9:15 And I will remember my covenant, which is between me and you and every living

creature of all flesh; and the waters shall no more become a flood to destroy all flesh. Can God

then change his mind?


Jer. 29:11 For I know the plans I have for you," declares the LORD, "plans to prosper you and

not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.


God has a perfect plan for every person's life, but He doesn't make us walk that path. We are

free moral agents with the ability to choose.


Deut. 30:19 I call heaven and earth to record this day against you, that I have set before you life

and death, blessing and cursing: therefore choose life, that both thou and thy seed may live…

He has told us what the right choices are, but He doesn't make those choices for us. God gave

us the power to control our destiny.

Will God do it for me?


Rom 2:11 For there is no respect of persons with God.


Eph 6:9  neither is there respect of persons with him.


Acts 10:34 God is no respecter of persons:

What is a testament/covenant? It is a last will and testament that Jesus has left us and if we can

find it in the word then it is ours.


Conclusion:

•    God cannot lie

•    God cannot change his mind once he makes a promise

•    God cannot alter the thing he has spoken

•    God has magnified his word above even his name

•    The word of God will never pass away

•    The word will always prosper in the thing that it was sent for

•    God has a plan for each of us, but ultimately it is up to us to allow God to work on our behalf

•    What God will do for one he will do for all.
